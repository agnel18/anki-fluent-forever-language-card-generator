# Phase 1: Research Documentation
# Language: Latvian (Latviešu valoda)
# Code: lv
# Family: Indo-European (Baltic branch)
# Script: Latin alphabet with diacritics (LTR)
# Word Order: SVO (flexible, but predominantly Subject-Verb-Object)

---

## 1. LANGUAGE OVERVIEW

### Classification
- **Family:** Indo-European → Baltic → Eastern Baltic
- **Sister language:** Lithuanian (closely related)
- **Script:** Latin-based with diacritics: ā, č, ē, ģ, ī, ķ, ļ, ņ, š, ū, ž
- **Speakers:** ~1.75 million native speakers (Latvia)
- **Official language:** Latvia (EU official language)

### Writing System
- **Direction:** Left-to-right (LTR)
- **Alphabet:** 33 letters (standard Latvian alphabet)
- **Diacritics:** Long vowels marked with macron (ā, ē, ī, ū); palatalized consonants (ģ, ķ, ļ, ņ); sibilants (č, š, ž)
- **Phonology:** Pitch-accent system (falling vs. level tone) but not written

### Key Typological Features
1. **Highly inflected** — nouns, adjectives, pronouns decline for case and number
2. **Seven-case system** — nominative, genitive, dative, accusative, instrumental, locative, vocative
3. **Two grammatical genders** — masculine and feminine (no neuter)
4. **Definite/indefinite adjective distinction** — adjectives have two forms
5. **Three noun declension classes** — organized by stem type
6. **Free word order** with SVO as default
7. **Prefixal aspect** — verbal aspect indicated through prefixes (similar to Slavic)
8. **Reflexive forms** — both verbs and nouns can take reflexive suffix -s/-ties

## 2. GRAMMATICAL INVENTORY

### Nouns (Lietvārds)
**Declension Classes:**
- **Class 1 (-s, -is):** Masculine nouns ending in -s or -is (vīrs "man", brālis "brother")
- **Class 2 (-us):** Masculine nouns ending in -us (tirgus "market")
- **Class 3 (-a, -e):** Feminine nouns ending in -a or -e (māte "mother", zeme "earth")
- **Class 4 (-s fem.):** Feminine nouns ending in -s (sirds "heart")
- **Class 5 (-iens):** Masculine nouns (akmens "stone")

**Cases (Locījumi):**
| Case | Latvian | Function | Example |
|------|---------|----------|---------|
| Nominative (nom.) | Nominatīvs | Subject | māja (house, subj.) |
| Genitive (gen.) | Ģenitīvs | Possession, partitive | mājas (of the house) |
| Dative (dat.) | Datīvs | Indirect object, beneficiary | mājai (to/for the house) |
| Accusative (acc.) | Akuzatīvs | Direct object | māju (the house, obj.) |
| Instrumental (inst.) | Instrumentālis | Means, manner (archaic/formal) | māju (rarely used today) |
| Locative (loc.) | Lokatīvs | Location | mājā (in/at the house) |
| Vocative (voc.) | Vokatīvs | Direct address | māja! (o house!) |

**Number:** Singular (vienskaitlis) and Plural (daudzskaitlis)
**No grammatical articles** — definiteness expressed through adjective forms and context

### Adjectives (Īpašības vārds)
**Two forms:**
- **Indefinite (nenoteiktā forma):** Short form — used predicatively or without reference to specific entity
  - e.g., *labs* (good-m), *laba* (good-f)
- **Definite (noteiktā forma):** Long form — used attributively before a known/specific noun
  - e.g., *labais* (the good-m), *labā* (the good-f)
- Adjectives agree with nouns in gender, number, and case
- Comparative: *labāks* (better); Superlative: *labākais* (the best)

### Verbs (Darbības vārds)
**Conjugation Classes (3 main classes):**
- Class 1: -āt infinitives (runāt "to speak")
- Class 2: -īt/-ināt infinitives (skriet "to run")
- Class 3: -ināt causatives

**Tenses:**
- Present (tagadne): *es runāju* (I speak/am speaking)
- Past (pagātne): *es runāju* (simple past — same form, context distinguishes)
- Future (nākotne): *es runāšu* (I will speak)

**Mood:**
- Indicative (īstenības izteiksme): factual statements
- Conditional (vēlējuma izteiksme): *es runātu* (I would speak)
- Debitive (vajadzības izteiksme): *man jārunā* (I must speak) — unique to Baltic

**Voice:**
- Active: regular conjugation
- Reflexive/Middle: -ties suffix — *sist* → *sisties* (to hit → to hit oneself)

**Participles (Divdabji):** Latvian has an extensive participial system:
- Present active participle: *runājošs* (speaking)
- Past active participle: *runājis* (having spoken)
- Present passive participle: *runājams* (to be spoken)
- Past passive participle: *runāts* (having been spoken)

**Debitive mood (vajadzības izteiksme):** Unique Latvian feature
- Impersonal construction: *Man jārunā* (I need to speak; literally "for-me need-to-speak")
- Expresses obligation/necessity

### Pronouns (Vietniekvārds)
- **Personal:** es, tu, viņš/viņa, mēs, jūs, viņi/viņas
- **Reflexive:** sevi (oneself — no nominative form)
- **Demonstrative:** šis/šī (this), tas/tā (that)
- **Interrogative:** kas (who/what), kurš/kura (which)
- **Relative:** kas, kurš/kura
- **Indefinite:** kāds/kāda (some), neviens/neviena (no one)
- **Possessive:** expressed through genitive pronouns: mana māja (my house)

### Determiners / Articles
- **No articles** — Latvian has no definite or indefinite articles
- **Demonstratives** function as determiners: šis galds (this table), tas galds (that/the table)
- **Definiteness** encoded in adjective form: *labs cilvēks* (a good person) vs *labais cilvēks* (the good person)

### Prepositions (Prievārds)
Common prepositions with case government:
- **Genitive:** bez (without), līdz (until/to), no (from), pēc (after/for), pie (at/near), priekš (for), starp (between)
- **Dative:** pret (against/toward), pretī (opposite)
- **Accusative:** ap (around), caur (through), gar (past/along), pār (over), par (about/for), uz (to/onto)
- **Locative:** aiz (behind), ap (around), iekš (inside), no (from), pa (along), pie (near), uz (on/at)

Note: Many prepositions govern multiple cases with different meanings.

### Conjunctions (Saiklis)
- **Coordinating:** un (and), bet (but), vai (or), jo (because), tāpēc (therefore)
- **Subordinating:** ka (that), kad (when), ja (if), lai (so that), kaut (although)
- **Correlative:** ne ... ne (neither ... nor), gan ... gan (both ... and)

### Adverbs (Apstākļa vārds)
- Many derived from adjectives: *labs* → *labi* (good → well)
- Manner: *labi* (well), *ātri* (quickly), *lēni* (slowly)
- Time: *tagad* (now), *tad* (then), *vienmēr* (always), *nekad* (never)
- Place: *šeit* (here), *tur* (there), *mājās* (at home)
- Degree: *ļoti* (very), *diezgan* (quite), *gandrīz* (almost)

### Numbers (Skaitļa vārds)
- Cardinal: viens/viena (one), divi/divas (two), trīs (three)
- Ordinal: decline like adjectives: pirmais/pirmā (first)

## 3. COMPLEXITY HIERARCHY

### Beginner Level
Core vocabulary and basic sentence structure:
- Basic nouns (nominative, genitive, accusative only)
- Present tense verbs (regular conjugation)
- Personal pronouns (subject forms)
- Common prepositions
- Basic adjectives (indefinite form only)
- Coordinating conjunctions (un, bet)
- Simple adverbs

### Intermediate Level
All beginner content plus:
- All 7 noun cases
- Past and future tense
- Definite vs. indefinite adjective forms
- Reflexive verbs (-ties)
- Relative and subordinating conjunctions
- Participles (basic)
- Debitive mood introduction
- All prepositions with their case government

### Advanced Level
All intermediate content plus:
- Full participial system (4 types)
- Conditional mood
- Debitive mood (full usage)
- Complex subordinate clauses
- Verbal nouns (darbības vārda lietvārds)
- Prefixal aspect distinctions
- Archaic/literary forms
- Idiomatic expressions
- Complex agreement rules

## 4. LATVIAN-SPECIFIC CHALLENGES FOR AI ANALYSIS

### Homographic Forms
- Same surface form can be different cases/genders
- *māju* = accusative singular of *māja* (house) OR genitive plural of *māja*
- Context and co-occurrence are essential for disambiguation

### Aspect via Prefixes
- Unlike Slavic languages, Latvian uses prefixes less systematically for aspect
- Common prefixes: *ie-*, *iz-*, *no-*, *pa-*, *par-*, *pār-*, *pie-*, *sa-*, *uz-*
- *lasīt* (to read/be reading) vs *izlasīt* (to read through/finish reading)

### Debitive Mood
- Construction: *man jā-* + infinitive stem
- Unique to Baltic; marks obligation
- AI must identify this as a separate mood category

### Participial Agreement
- Latvian participles agree with the noun they modify in gender, number, case
- Active participles: *strādājošais vīrs* (the working man), *strādājošā sieviete* (the working woman)

### Definiteness in Adjectives
- The distinction between definite and indefinite adjective forms is critical
- *Jauns vīrs atnāca.* (A young man came.) — indefinite
- *Jaunais vīrs atnāca.* (The young man came.) — definite
- AI must identify which form is used and why

### Stress Patterns
- Stress usually falls on the first syllable
- Long vowels (ā, ē, ī, ū) affect pronunciation but are always written

## 6. SAMPLE SENTENCES FOR VALIDATION

### Beginner
- *Es runāju latviski.* (I speak Latvian.)
- *Māja ir skaista.* (The house is beautiful.)
- *Viņš iet uz skolu.* (He goes to school.)

### Intermediate
- *Mana māte gatavo garšīgu vakariņas.* (My mother prepares a delicious dinner.)
- *Bērniem patīk spēlēties parkā.* (Children like to play in the park.)
- *Man jāmācās latviešu valoda.* (I must study Latvian language.) — debitive

### Advanced
- *Uzrakstītā vēstule tika nosūtīta vakar.* (The written letter was sent yesterday.)
- *Strādājošais skolotājs, kuru es pazīstu, dzīvo kaimiņos.* (The working teacher whom I know lives nearby.)
- *Ja viņš būtu studējis vairāk, viņš būtu nokārtojis eksāmenu.* (If he had studied more, he would have passed the exam.)
