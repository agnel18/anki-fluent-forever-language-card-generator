# Version: 2026-01-19 (Following Hindi Gold Standard Template)

## Language Overview
- **Family**: Sino-Tibetan
- **Script**: Simplified Chinese characters (汉字简化字), Left-to-Right (LTR) writing direction
- **Complexity**: Analytic language - relies on word order, particles, and context rather than inflectional morphology
- **Key Characteristics**: Topic-prominent language, aspect-based tense system, obligatory classifiers for counting, monosyllabic roots with compounding

## Grammatical Categories

### Parts of Speech (词类)
Chinese has 11 major word classes, but for grammar analysis, we focus on functional categories:

#### 1. Noun (名词 - míngcí)
- **Characteristics**: No gender, number, or case inflection
- **Examples**: 书 (shū - book), 学生 (xuésheng - student), 北京 (Běijīng - Beijing)
- **Special Types**: Proper nouns, common nouns, abstract nouns
- **Classifier Requirement**: Must use classifiers when counted (一本书 - yī běn shū)

#### 2. Verb (动词 - dòngcí)
- **Characteristics**: No tense, person, or number inflection
- **Aspect Marking**: Uses particles for completion (了), duration (着), experience (过)
- **Examples**: 吃 (chī - eat), 学习 (xuéxí - study), 去 (qù - go)
- **Compound Verbs**: Resultative compounds (吃完 - chī wán - eat finish), directional compounds (进来 - jìn lái - come in)

#### 3. Adjective (形容词 - xíngróngcí)
- **Characteristics**: Can function as predicates without linking verbs
- **Degree Modification**: Uses 很 (hěn - very) for intensification
- **Examples**: 大 (dà - big), 漂亮 (piàoliang - beautiful), 红 (hóng - red)
- **Stative Verbs**: Many adjectives can take aspect particles like verbs

#### 4. Adverb (副词 - fùcí)
- **Characteristics**: Modifies verbs, adjectives, or other adverbs
- **Position**: Usually before the modified element
- **Examples**: 很 (hěn - very), 不 (bù - not), 也 (yě - also), 都 (dōu - all)

#### 5. Numeral (数词 - shùcí)
- **Characteristics**: Cardinal and ordinal numbers
- **Examples**: 一 (yī - one), 二 (èr - two), 第一 (dì yī - first)
- **Usage**: Always requires classifiers for counting nouns

#### 6. Classifier/Measure Word (量词 - liàngcí)
- **Characteristics**: Obligatory for counting nouns, indicates shape/size/unit
- **Examples**: 个 (gè - general), 本 (běn - for books), 杯 (bēi - cup), 只 (zhī - for animals)
- **Function**: Categorizes nouns by physical properties

#### 7. Pronoun (代词 - dàicí)
- **Characteristics**: Personal, demonstrative, interrogative
- **Examples**: 我 (wǒ - I), 你 (nǐ - you), 这 (zhè - this), 谁 (shuí - who)
- **Special**: No gender distinction in third person singular

#### 8. Preposition (介词 - jiècí) / Coverb
- **Characteristics**: Indicates location, time, manner
- **Examples**: 在 (zài - at/in), 从 (cóng - from), 给 (gěi - to/for)
- **Function**: Forms prepositional phrases

#### 9. Conjunction (连词 - liáncí)
- **Characteristics**: Connects clauses or sentences
- **Examples**: 和 (hé - and), 但是 (dànshì - but), 因为 (yīnwei - because)
- **Types**: Coordinating, subordinating

#### 10. Particle (助词 - zhùcí)
- **Characteristics**: Grammatical function words, no independent meaning
- **Types**:
  - Structural: 的 (de - possessive/modifier), 地 (de - adverbial), 得 (de - complement)
  - Aspect: 了 (le - completed), 着 (zhe - ongoing), 过 (guo - experienced)
  - Modal: 吗 (ma - question), 呢 (ne - topic continuation), 吧 (ba - suggestion)

#### 11. Interjection (叹词 - tàncí)
- **Characteristics**: Exclamations
- **Examples**: 啊 (a), 哦 (o), 哎呀 (āiyā)

## Key Features

### 1. Topic-Comment Structure (主题-述题结构)
- **Primary Structure**: Topic + Comment rather than Subject + Predicate
- **Example**: 这本书很有意思 (Zhè běn shū hěn yǒu yìsi) - This book is very interesting
  - Topic: 这本书 (this book)
  - Comment: 很有意思 (is very interesting)
- **Implication**: Word order is flexible, subject can be omitted if topicalized

### 2. Aspect System (体貌系统)
- **No Tense**: Time reference through context, time words, aspect particles
- **Aspect Particles**:
  - 了 (le): Perfective aspect - completed action
  - 着 (zhe): Progressive/durative aspect - ongoing action
  - 过 (guo): Experiential aspect - experienced action
- **Example**: 我吃了饭 (Wǒ chī le fàn) - I ate (completed)

### 3. Classifier System (量词系统)
- **Obligatory**: Required for counting nouns
- **Semantic Classification**: Classifiers categorize nouns by shape, function, or animacy
- **Examples**:
  - 一本书 (yī běn shū) - one book (本 for bound objects)
  - 一只猫 (yī zhī māo) - one cat (只 for animals)
  - 一杯水 (yī bēi shuǐ) - one cup of water (杯 for containers)

### 4. Word Formation (构词法)
- **Compounding**: Most common method - noun + noun, verb + object, etc.
- **Examples**: 火车 (huǒ chē - fire vehicle = train), 吃饭 (chī fàn - eat rice = eat)
- **Affixation**: Limited - prefixes like 老 (lǎo - old), suffixes like 子 (zi - diminutive)

### 5. Sentence Structure (句法结构)
- **Basic Word Order**: Subject-Verb-Object (SVO)
- **Modifier Position**: Before modified element (adjective-noun, adverb-verb)
- **Serial Verbs**: Multiple verbs without conjunctions
- **Example**: 我去商店买东西 (Wǒ qù shāngdiàn mǎi dōngxī) - I go store buy things

### 6. Negation (否定)
- **Main Negator**: 不 (bù) - for general negation
- **Past Negator**: 没 (méi) - for uncompleted actions
- **Examples**:
  - 我不去 (Wǒ bù qù) - I don't go
  - 我没去 (Wǒ méi qù) - I didn't go

### 7. Question Formation (疑问句)
- **Particle Questions**: Add 吗 (ma) to statements
- **Example**: 你去吗？ (Nǐ qù ma?) - Do you go?
- **Alternative Questions**: Use 还是 (háishi)
- **Wh-Questions**: Use question words like 什么 (shénme - what), 谁 (shuí - who)

## Script-Specific Considerations

### 1. Character System (汉字系统)
- **Logographic**: Each character represents a morpheme
- **Polysyllabic Words**: Most content words are compounds of 2-4 characters
- **Homophones**: Many characters sound the same but mean different things
- **Unicode Range**: U+4E00-U+9FFF for common characters

### 2. Pinyin Romanization (拼音)
- **Official System**: Hanyu Pinyin
- **Tone Marks**: Four tones (ā, á, ǎ, à) plus neutral (a)
- **Syllable Structure**: Initial + Final + Tone
- **Usage**: For input, pronunciation, and learners

### 3. Tonal System (声调系统)
- **Four Tones**: High (¯), rising (´), falling-rising (ˇ), falling (`)
- **Neutral Tone**: Unmarked, depends on context
- **Tone Sandhi**: Tone changes in connected speech
- **Importance**: Tones distinguish meaning (mā mā mà ma - mother, hemp, horse, scold)

### 4. Character Processing Challenges
- **Segmentation**: No spaces between words - requires word segmentation
- **Ambiguity**: Multiple possible segmentations
- **Compound Recognition**: Identify multi-character words as units
- **Variant Forms**: Traditional vs. Simplified characters

## Grammatical Analysis Requirements

### Core Categories for Analyzer
Based on pedagogical needs for language learners:

1. **noun** - 名词 (common nouns, proper nouns)
2. **verb** - 动词 (action words, stative verbs)
3. **adjective** - 形容词 (descriptive words)
4. **adverb** - 副词 (modifiers of verbs/adjectives)
5. **pronoun** - 代词 (substitutes for nouns)
6. **numeral** - 数词 (numbers)
7. **classifier** - 量词 (measure words)
8. **particle** - 助词 (grammatical particles: 的, 了, 着, 过, 吗, etc.)
9. **preposition** - 介词 (relational words: 在, 从, 给, etc.)
10. **conjunction** - 连词 (connecting words: 和, 但是, 因为, etc.)

### Special Chinese-Specific Categories
11. **aspect_marker** - 体貌标记 (了, 着, 过)
12. **modal_particle** - 语气助词 (呢, 吧, 啊, 吗)
13. **structural_particle** - 结构助词 (的, 地, 得)

### Word Ordering Considerations
- **LTR Script**: Explanations should appear in sentence word order (left to right)
- **Compound Words**: Treat multi-character compounds as single units
- **Particle Attachment**: Particles are separate from content words

### Validation Patterns
- **Aspect Particle Usage**: 了 with completed actions, 着 with ongoing, 过 with experienced
- **Classifier Agreement**: Appropriate classifiers for nouns
- **Word Order**: SVO basic structure, modifiers before modified
- **Negation**: 不 vs. 没 distinction
- **Question Formation**: Proper particle usage

### IPA Integration Strategy
- **Strict IPA Required**: Chinese is tonal, so romanization alone insufficient
- **Pinyin with Tones**: Acceptable for learners, but full IPA preferred
- **Tone Representation**: Include tone numbers or marks in IPA
- **Syllable Structure**: CV, CVC patterns with tone

## Implementation Architecture Decisions

### Base Class Selection
- **BaseGrammarAnalyzer**: Chinese differs significantly from Indo-European
- **No Tense/Person/Number Inflection**: Cannot use IndoEuropeanAnalyzer patterns
- **Analytic Structure**: Focus on particles, classifiers, aspect marking

### Key Implementation Components
1. **Pattern Recognition**: Aspect particles, classifiers, structural particles
2. **Word Segmentation**: Handle character compounds properly
3. **Aspect Validation**: Correct usage of 了/着/过
4. **Classifier Validation**: Appropriate measure words
5. **Tone Integration**: Include tone information where available

### Batch Processing Considerations
- **8-Sentence Batches**: Standard limit for complete responses
- **4000 max_tokens**: Required for detailed Chinese analysis
- **Character-Based Analysis**: Consider both character and word levels
- **Compound Word Handling**: Identify and treat compounds as units

### Error Handling Strategy
- **Fallback Patterns**: Rule-based aspect and particle validation
- **Confidence Scoring**: Penalize missing classifiers, incorrect aspect usage
- **Graceful Degradation**: Provide basic analysis even with segmentation issues
