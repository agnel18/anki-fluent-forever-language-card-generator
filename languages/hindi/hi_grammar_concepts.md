# Version: 2026-01-19 (Following Chinese Gold Standard Template)

## Language Overview
- **Family**: Indo-European (Indo-Aryan branch)
- **Script**: Devanagari (देवनागरी), Left-to-Right (LTR) writing direction
- **Complexity**: Fusional language - uses inflectional morphology for case, gender, number, tense
- **Key Characteristics**: Split ergativity, verb agreement with subjects/objects, postpositions, honorific system, retroflex consonants

## Grammatical Categories

### Parts of Speech (शब्द-भेद)
Hindi has 8 major word classes, but for grammar analysis, we focus on functional categories:

#### 1. Noun (संज्ञा - saṃjñā)
- **Characteristics**: Gender (masculine/feminine), number (singular/plural), case inflection
- **Examples**: लड़का (larka - boy), लड़की (larki - girl), किताब (kitab - book)
- **Special Types**: Proper nouns, common nouns, abstract nouns, compound nouns
- **Inflection**: Changes form based on case (nominative, accusative, dative, etc.)

#### 2. Verb (क्रिया - kriyā)
- **Characteristics**: Person, number, gender agreement, tense, aspect, mood
- **Inflection**: Complex conjugation system with root + suffixes
- **Examples**: खाना (khanā - to eat), जाना (jānā - to go), लिखना (likhnā - to write)
- **Compound Forms**: Auxiliary verbs (होना - honā), modal verbs (सकना - saknā)

#### 3. Adjective (विशेषण - viśeṣaṇ)
- **Characteristics**: Gender, number, case agreement with nouns
- **Position**: Usually precedes noun, can follow in some constructions
- **Examples**: अच्छा (acchā - good), बड़ा (baṛā - big), सुंदर (sundar - beautiful)
- **Degrees**: Positive, comparative (से - se), superlative (सबसे - sabse)

#### 4. Pronoun (सर्वनाम - sarvnām)
- **Characteristics**: Person (1st/2nd/3rd), gender, number, case
- **Examples**: मैं (main - I), तू (tū - you informal), आप (āp - you formal), वह (vah - he/she/it)
- **Special**: Honorific distinctions, reflexive pronouns (स्वयं - svayaṃ)

#### 5. Postposition (विभक्ति - vibhakti)
- **Characteristics**: Indicates grammatical relations, follows nouns/pronouns
- **Examples**: का (kā - of), को (ko - to), से (se - from/with), में (meṃ - in)
- **Function**: Equivalent to prepositions in English but postpositional

#### 6. Adverb (क्रिया-विशेषण - kriyā-viśeṣaṇ)
- **Characteristics**: Modifies verbs, adjectives, or other adverbs
- **Position**: Usually before verb, can be sentence-initial
- **Examples**: बहुत (bahut - very), जल्दी (jaldī - quickly), धीरे (dhīre - slowly)

#### 7. Conjunction (संयोजक - saṃyojak)
- **Characteristics**: Connects clauses or sentences
- **Examples**: और (aur - and), पर (par - but), क्योंकि (kyoṅki - because)
- **Types**: Coordinating (और, पर), subordinating (क्योंकि, अगर)

#### 8. Interjection (विस्मयादिबोधक - vismayādibodhak)
- **Characteristics**: Expresses emotions or calls attention
- **Examples**: अरे (are), हाय (hāy), वाह (vāh)

## Key Features

### 1. Gender Agreement (लिंग-समन्वय)
- **Noun Classification**: Masculine (-ā ending) vs Feminine (-ī/ī ending)
- **Agreement**: Adjectives, verbs agree with noun gender
- **Example**: अच्छा लड़का (acchā larkā - good boy), अच्छी लड़की (acchī larkī - good girl)

### 2. Case System (कारक-व्यवस्था)
- **Eight Cases**: Direct, oblique, and postpositional combinations
- **Inflection**: Nouns/pronouns change form in oblique case
- **Postpositions**: Attach to oblique forms to show grammatical relations

### 3. Verb Conjugation (क्रिया-विकरण)
- **Person/Number**: 1st/2nd/3rd person, singular/plural
- **Tense**: Present, past, future
- **Aspect**: Perfective, imperfective, habitual
- **Gender**: Past tense verbs agree with subject gender

### 4. Split Ergativity (विभाजित-कर्तृता)
- **Nominative-Accusative**: Present tense uses nominative subjects
- **Ergative-Absolutive**: Perfective past uses ergative (ने - ne) subjects
- **Example**: लड़का खाना खाता है (larkā khānā khātā hai - boy eats food)
- **Example**: लड़के ने खाना खाया (larke ne khānā khāyā - boy ate food)

### 5. Honorific System (सम्मान-व्यवस्था)
- **Pronouns**: तू (informal), तुम (semi-formal), आप (formal)
- **Verbs**: Different forms for different levels of respect
- **Example**: जाना (jānā - to go), जाएं (jāeṃ - go with respect)

### 6. Word Formation (शब्द-रचना)
- **Compounding**: Noun + noun, noun + adjective combinations
- **Derivation**: Adding prefixes/suffixes (-ता, -ना, -आ)
- **Examples**: रेलगाड़ी (relgāṛī - train), खुशी (khushī - happiness)

### 7. Sentence Structure (वाक्य-संरचना)
- **Word Order**: Subject-Object-Verb (SOV) in neutral sentences
- **Flexibility**: Topic-prominent language allows fronting
- **Example**: लड़का खाना खाता है (larkā khānā khātā hai - boy food eats)

### 8. Negation (नकार)
- **Main Negator**: नहीं (nahīṃ) - general negation
- **Past Negator**: न (na) - with perfective verbs
- **Examples**: नहीं जाता (nahīṃ jātā - doesn't go), न गया (na gayā - didn't go)

## Script-Specific Considerations

### 1. Devanagari Script (देवनागरी लिपि)
- **Abugida**: Consonants have inherent vowel, diacritics modify vowels
- **Conjuncts**: Consonant clusters represented by ligatures
- **Retroflex**: Special retroflex consonants (ट, ठ, ड, ढ, ण)
- **Unicode Range**: U+0900-U+097F for Devanagari

### 2. Romanization (रोमन लिप्यंतरण)
- **Standard System**: IAST (International Alphabet of Sanskrit Transliteration)
- **Common Variants**: Harvard-Kyoto, ITRANS
- **Diacritics**: ṃ (anusvāra), ṅ (ṅa), ñ (ña), etc.

### 3. Word Segmentation Challenges
- **Sandhi**: Sound changes at word boundaries
- **Compounding**: Multi-word expressions treated as single units
- **Ambiguity**: Multiple possible segmentations
- **No Spaces**: Traditional script doesn't use spaces between words

## Grammatical Analysis Requirements

### Core Categories for Analyzer
Based on pedagogical needs for language learners:

1. **noun** - संज्ञा (common nouns, proper nouns)
2. **verb** - क्रिया (action words, auxiliary verbs)
3. **adjective** - विशेषण (descriptive words)
4. **pronoun** - सर्वनाम (personal, demonstrative, reflexive)
5. **postposition** - विभक्ति (grammatical relation markers)
6. **adverb** - क्रिया-विशेषण (verb modifiers)
7. **conjunction** - संयोजक (connectors)
8. **interjection** - विस्मयादिबोधक (exclamation words)

### Special Hindi-Specific Categories
9. **auxiliary_verb** - सहायक क्रिया (होना, होना, etc.)
10. **case_marker** - कारक चिह्न (ने, को, से, etc.)
11. **honorific_marker** - सम्मान चिह्न (respect markers)

### Word Ordering Considerations
- **LTR Script**: Explanations should appear in sentence word order (left to right)
- **Pronoun before Noun**: In Hindi, pronouns typically come before nouns in noun phrases
- **Postposition Attachment**: Postpositions are separate from nouns but follow them
- **Verb Final**: Verbs typically come at the end of clauses

### Validation Patterns
- **Gender Agreement**: Adjectives/verbs must agree with noun gender
- **Case Marking**: Correct postposition usage with oblique forms
- **Ergativity**: ने (ne) particle with perfective transitive verbs
- **Honorifics**: Appropriate pronoun/verb forms for respect levels
- **Word Order**: SOV structure in neutral sentences

### IPA Integration Strategy
- **Strict IPA Required**: Hindi has retroflex and aspirated consonants
- **IAST Preferred**: Academic standard for Hindi romanization
- **Retroflex Representation**: Use IPA symbols (ʈ, ɖ, ɳ, etc.)
- **Aspiration**: Include aspiration diacritics (ʈʰ, dʱ, etc.)

## Implementation Architecture Decisions

### Base Class Selection
- **IndoEuropeanAnalyzer**: Hindi is fusional with inflectional morphology
- **Gender/Case/Agreement**: Cannot use BaseGrammarAnalyzer patterns

1. **Agreement Validation**: Gender/number/case agreement checking
2. **Case System**: Postposition and oblique form validation
3. **Ergativity Patterns**: ने (ne) particle usage validation
4. **Honorific System**: Appropriate form selection
5. **Sandhi Handling**: Word boundary sound changes

### Batch Processing Considerations
- **8-Sentence Batches**: Standard limit for complete responses
- **4000 max_tokens**: Required for detailed Hindi analysis
- **Character-Based Analysis**: Consider conjuncts and diacritics
- **Agreement Complexity**: Higher processing needs for agreement validation

### Error Handling Strategy
- **Fallback Patterns**: Rule-based agreement and case validation
- **Confidence Scoring**: Penalize agreement errors, incorrect case usage
- **Graceful Degradation**: Provide basic analysis even with segmentation issues
