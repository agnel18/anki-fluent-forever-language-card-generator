## üìö Core Grammatical Categories

### 1. Cases (Kasus) - The Foundation of German Grammar
German has **four grammatical cases** that determine article, adjective, and pronoun forms:

#### Nominativ (Nominative) - Subject Case
- **Function:** Marks the subject of a sentence
- **Articles:** der, die, das
- **Example:** "Der Mann isst einen Apfel." (The man eats an apple.)
- **Usage:** Subject of main clauses, predicate nominative

#### Akkusativ (Accusative) - Direct Object Case
- **Function:** Marks direct objects and some prepositional objects
- **Articles:** den, die, das
- **Example:** "Ich sehe den Mann." (I see the man.)
- **Usage:** Direct objects, accusative prepositions (durch, f√ºr, gegen, ohne, um)

#### Dativ (Dative) - Indirect Object Case
- **Function:** Marks indirect objects and possession
- **Articles:** dem, der, dem
- **Example:** "Ich gebe dem Mann einen Apfel." (I give the man an apple.)
- **Usage:** Indirect objects, dative prepositions (aus, bei, mit, nach, von, zu)

#### Genitiv (Genitive) - Possessive Case
- **Function:** Marks possession and relationships
- **Articles:** des, der, des
- **Example:** "Das Auto des Mannes" (The man's car)
- **Usage:** Possession, partitive constructions, some prepositions (wegen, trotz, w√§hrend)

### 2. Genders (Genera) - Three Gender System
German nouns have **grammatical gender** independent of biological gender:

#### Maskulinum (Masculine)
- **Definite Article:** der (Nom), den (Akk), dem (Dat), des (Gen)
- **Typical Endings:** -ig, -ling, -ismus, -en (weak nouns)
- **Examples:** der Mann (man), der Hund (dog), der Computer (computer)

#### Femininum (Feminine)
- **Definite Article:** die (Nom/Akk), der (Dat), der (Gen)
- **Typical Endings:** -ung, -heit, -keit, -schaft, -ei, -in
- **Examples:** die Frau (woman), die Katze (cat), die Zeitung (newspaper)

#### Neutrum (Neuter)
- **Definite Article:** das (Nom/Akk), dem (Dat), des (Gen)
- **Typical Endings:** -chen, -lein, -ment, -um, -tum
- **Examples:** das Kind (child), das Buch (book), das Haus (house)

## üèóÔ∏è Sentence Structure and Word Order

### 1. Basic Word Order - V2 Principle (Verb Second)
German follows strict **V2 word order** in main clauses:
- **Position 1:** Subject or other element
- **Position 2:** Finite verb (conjugated verb)
- **Rest:** Other elements in various orders

**Examples:**
- "Ich esse einen Apfel." (I eat an apple.)
- "Einen Apfel esse ich." (An apple I eat.)
- "Morgen esse ich einen Apfel." (Tomorrow I eat an apple.)

### 2. Subordinate Clauses - Verb Final
In subordinate clauses, the finite verb moves to the end:
- **Main:** "Ich wei√ü, dass du kommst."
- **Subordinate:** "dass du kommst" (verb at end)

### 3. Question Word Order
Questions invert subject and verb:
- **Yes/No:** "Kommst du?" (Are you coming?)
- **W-questions:** "Wann kommst du?" (When are you coming?)

## üé≠ Mood and Complex Constructions

### 1. Subjunctive Mood (Konjunktiv)

#### Konjunktiv I - Indirect Speech/Reporting
Used for reported speech and formal indirect statements:
- **Present:** "Er sagt, sie komme." (He says she is coming.)
- **Past:** "Er sagte, sie sei gekommen." (He said she had come.)
- **Would (conditional):** "Er w√ºrde kommen." (He would come.)

**Formation:** Add -e to infinitive stem + subjunctive endings

#### Konjunktiv II - Hypothetical/Irrealis
Used for unreal conditions, wishes, and polite requests:
- **Present:** "Wenn ich reich w√§re..." (If I were rich...)
- **Past:** "Wenn ich reich gewesen w√§re..." (If I had been rich...)
- **Polite:** "H√§tten Sie Zeit?" (Would you have time?)

**Formation:** Mostly from preterite forms + subjunctive endings

### 2. Passive Voice (Passiv)

#### Vorgangspassiv (Process Passive)
Describes an action happening:
- **Present:** "Das Buch wird gelesen." (The book is being read.)
- **Past:** "Das Buch wurde gelesen." (The book was read.)
- **Future:** "Das Buch wird gelesen werden." (The book will be read.)

#### Zustandspassiv (State Passive)
Describes a state resulting from an action:
- **Present:** "Das Buch ist gelesen." (The book is read/finished.)
- **Past:** "Das Buch war gelesen." (The book was read/finished.)

**Formation:** werden + past participle (position 2 in main clauses)

### 3. Relative Clauses (Relativs√§tze)
Clauses that modify nouns, introduced by relative pronouns:

**Relative pronouns (agree with antecedent in gender/case):**
- **der/die/das** (who/which/that) - changes case
- **welcher/welche/welches** (which) - more formal
- **was** (what/which) - for general antecedents

**Examples:**
- **Nom:** "Der Mann, der dort steht..." (The man who is standing there...)
- **Akk:** "Die Frau, die ich sehe..." (The woman whom I see...)
- **Dat:** "Das Kind, dem ich helfe..." (The child whom I help to...)

### 4. Reflexive Verbs (Reflexive Verben)
Verbs that require a reflexive pronoun (sich):

**True reflexive:** Action directed at oneself
- **sich waschen** (to wash oneself) ‚Üí "Ich wasche mich"
- **sich anziehen** (to get dressed) ‚Üí "Er zieht sich an"

**False reflexive:** Idiomatic usage
- **sich freuen** (to be happy) ‚Üí "Ich freue mich"
- **sich beeilen** (to hurry) ‚Üí "Wir beeilen uns"

**Reflexive pronouns:**
- **Akk:** mich, dich, sich, uns, euch, sich
- **Dat:** mir, dir, sich, uns, euch, sich

#### zu + Infinitive
Used after certain verbs and in final clauses:
- **Nach verbs:** vergessen, versprechen, versuchen

## üîç Common Learner Difficulties

- **V2 principle:** Verb always second in main clauses
- **Middle field scrambling:** Flexible order between verb and end
- **Clause-final verbs:** Complex subordinate structures
