## Language Overview
- **Family**: Sino-Tibetan
- **Script**: Traditional Chinese characters (繁體字), Left-to-Right (LTR) writing direction
- **Complexity**: Analytic language - relies on word order, particles, and context rather than inflectional morphology
- **Key Characteristics**: Topic-prominent language, aspect-based tense system, obligatory classifiers for counting, monosyllabic roots with compounding

## Grammatical Categories

### Parts of Speech (詞類)
Chinese has 11 major word classes, but for grammar analysis, we focus on functional categories:

#### 1. Noun (名詞 - míngcí)
- **Characteristics**: No gender, number, or case inflection
- **Examples**: 書 (shū - book), 學生 (xuésheng - student), 北京 (Běijīng - Beijing)
- **Special Types**: Proper nouns, common nouns, abstract nouns
- **Classifier Requirement**: Must use classifiers when counted (一本書 - yī běn shū)

#### 2. Verb (動詞 - dòngcí)
- **Characteristics**: No tense, person, or number inflection
- **Aspect Marking**: Uses particles for completion (了), duration (著), experience (過)
- **Examples**: 吃 (chī - eat), 學習 (xuéxí - study), 去 (qù - go)
- **Compound Verbs**: Resultative compounds (吃完 - chī wán - eat finish), directional compounds (進來 - jìn lái - come in)

#### 3. Adjective (形容詞 - xíngróngcí)
- **Characteristics**: Can function as predicates without linking verbs
- **Degree Modification**: Uses 很 (hěn - very) for intensification
- **Examples**: 大 (dà - big), 漂亮 (piàoliang - beautiful), 紅 (hóng - red)
- **Stative Verbs**: Many adjectives can take aspect particles like verbs

#### 4. Adverb (副詞 - fùcí)
- **Characteristics**: Modifies verbs, adjectives, or other adverbs
- **Position**: Usually before the modified element
- **Examples**: 很 (hěn - very), 不 (bù - not), 也 (yě - also), 都 (dōu - all)

#### 5. Numeral (數詞 - shùcí)
- **Characteristics**: Cardinal and ordinal numbers
- **Examples**: 一 (yī - one), 二 (èr - two), 第一 (dì yī - first)
- **Usage**: Always requires classifiers for counting nouns

#### 6. Classifier/Measure Word (量詞 - liàngcí)
- **Characteristics**: Obligatory for counting nouns, indicates shape/size/unit
- **Examples**: 個 (gè - general), 本 (běn - for books), 杯 (bēi - cup), 隻 (zhī - for animals)
- **Function**: Categorizes nouns by physical properties

#### 7. Pronoun (代詞 - dàicí)
- **Characteristics**: Personal, demonstrative, interrogative
- **Examples**: 我 (wǒ - I), 你 (nǐ - you), 這 (zhè - this), 誰 (shuí - who)
