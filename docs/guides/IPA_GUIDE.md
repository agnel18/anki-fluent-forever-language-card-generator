## What is IPA?

**IPA (International Phonetic Alphabet)** is the universal system for phonetic transcription used by linguists, language teachers, and dictionaries worldwide. It provides a standardized way to represent the sounds of any spoken language using special symbols.

## Why Learn IPA?

### üéØ **Accurate Pronunciation**
- Learn exactly how native speakers pronounce words
- Avoid common pronunciation mistakes
- Master sounds that don't exist in your native language

### üîÑ **Consistent Learning**
- Same symbols mean the same sounds across all languages
- Build a universal "sound library" in your mind
- Transfer pronunciation skills between languages

### üìñ **Scientific Standard**
- Used in academic linguistics and language teaching
- Found in quality dictionaries and textbooks
- Recognized by the International Phonetic Association

## Tips for Learning IPA

### 1. **Start Small**
- Focus on one symbol at a time
- Practice with familiar words first
- Use ipachart.com to hear each sound

### 2. **Compare Languages**
- Note how your native language represents sounds
- Identify gaps in your phonetic awareness
- Practice "foreign" sounds daily

### 3. **Use in Context**
- Look at IPA in your Anki cards
- Say words out loud while checking IPA
- Record yourself and compare

### 4. **Be Patient**
- IPA is a skill that improves with practice
- Start with consonants, then vowels, then diphthongs
- Regular practice (10-15 minutes daily) yields results

## Common IPA Symbols

### Consonants
- `p` = "p" (as in "pat")
- `t` = "t" (as in "tap")
- `k` = "k" (as in "cat")
- `m` = "m" (as in "mat")
- `n` = "n" (as in "nap")
- `s` = "s" (as in "sat")
- `Œ∏` = "th" (as in "think")
- `√∞` = "th" (as in "this")

### Vowels
- `i` = "ee" (as in "machine")
- `u` = "oo" (as in "food")
- `…ô` = "uh" (as in "about")
- `…ë` = "ah" (as in "father")
- `…î` = "aw" (as in "law")
